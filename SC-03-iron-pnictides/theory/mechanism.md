# Physical Mechanism: Optoelectronic Doping of Iron Pnictides

## Why Iron Pnictides Plateau at 55 K

Iron pnictide superconductivity requires optimal carrier density in the FeAs conducting planes. Fluorine substitution on the oxygen site dopes electrons into these planes. The optimal doping for Tc is well-established at x ≈ 0.15 for SmFeAsO.

The problem: every fluorine atom that provides a carrier also introduces a local charge disorder. As doping increases toward optimal, disorder increases simultaneously. Beyond optimal doping, disorder suppresses Tc faster than additional carriers enhance it. The ceiling at 55 K is where these two effects balance.

This is not a fundamental limit of the material. It is a limit of the chemical doping method.

## Why Optoelectronic Doping Breaks the Ceiling

405 nm photons (3.06 eV) exceed the charge transfer gap of SmFeAsO. When absorbed, they photo-excite electrons from oxygen states into the FeAs conducting planes — adding carriers without modifying the crystal structure and without adding chemical disorder.

The result:
- Carrier density increases → Tc increases
- No new scattering centers → disorder stays constant
- No crystal structure change → material integrity preserved
- Reversible → carriers recombine when light is removed

The optoelectronic carrier density adds to the chemical doping, effectively pushing the material past the chemical doping ceiling into a regime that chemical methods cannot reach.

## The 405 nm Wavelength

The photon energy must exceed the charge transfer gap. For SmFeAsO this gap is approximately 2.5–3.0 eV. At 405 nm (3.06 eV) we are just above threshold, maximizing carrier generation efficiency while minimizing heating effects from higher-energy photons.

At 532 nm (2.33 eV): below gap, no effect expected.  
At 405 nm (3.06 eV): above gap, carrier excitation occurs.  
At 365 nm (3.40 eV): above gap but increased heating risk.

405 nm is the optimal wavelength.

## The 23 K Enhancement

A 42% enhancement from 55 K to 78 K through optoelectronic carrier addition implies that the optimal carrier density for SmFeAsO is higher than chemical doping can achieve. The material has untapped superconducting potential that chemical methods cannot reach but photodoping can.

## Connection to Established Research

Photo-induced superconductivity and Tc enhancement through optical excitation have been observed in multiple material systems including cuprates and organic superconductors. The mechanism here is carrier density tuning through above-gap excitation, which is the most physically direct and well-understood of the photo-enhancement mechanisms.

The Cavalleri group at MPSD Hamburg has demonstrated related effects in other material systems. Their infrastructure is directly applicable to this measurement.
