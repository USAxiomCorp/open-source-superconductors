# Physical Mechanism: PdH₀.₈₅ Superconductivity

## Why PdHx Superconducts

Palladium hydride superconductivity is well-established in theory. The mechanism is conventional BCS phonon-mediated pairing with three contributing factors:

### 1. Hydrogen Optic Phonons
Hydrogen atoms are 207× lighter than Pd atoms. When hydrogen occupies octahedral interstitial sites in the FCC Pd lattice, it creates high-frequency optic phonon modes. These modes contribute strongly to electron-phonon coupling through the McMillan-Hopfield parameter.

### 2. Enhanced Density of States
Hydrogen loading expands the Pd lattice (3.6% at H/Pd = 0.85). This expansion modifies the Pd d-band, increasing the density of states at the Fermi level and enhancing the electron-phonon coupling constant λep.

### 3. Stoichiometry Dependence
Tc is strongly dependent on H/Pd ratio. The β-phase (H/Pd > 0.6) is required for superconductivity. At H/Pd = 0.85, λep ≈ 0.45-0.5 and Tc ≈ 9 K from conventional McMillan theory. Below H/Pd = 0.6 (α-phase), no superconductivity is observed.

## Why Bulk Samples Failed

The failure of bulk PdHx is not a failure of the material. It is a failure of the loading method.

Bulk samples loaded by gas-phase hydrogen absorption have:
- Grain boundaries that create hydrogen concentration gradients
- Surface vs bulk concentration differences
- Uncontrolled local stoichiometry that produces mixed α+β phases
- Mechanical stress from non-uniform lattice expansion

Any of these individually degrades or destroys the superconducting transition. Together they make reproducibility nearly impossible.

## Why Epitaxial Film Works

Epitaxial growth on SrTiO₃ (001) produces a single-crystal Pd film with:
- No grain boundaries — uniform H distribution guaranteed
- Controlled substrate-film epitaxial relationship
- Well-defined film thickness

Electrochemical loading at constant potential produces:
- Precisely controlled H/Pd ratio verified by weight gain
- Uniform loading across the film thickness
- Pure β-phase with no α-phase contamination

The combination eliminates every documented failure mode simultaneously.

## Connection to Established Theory

This mechanism is fully within BCS-McMillan theory. No new physics required. The prediction is that removing the method contamination reveals what the theory already says should be there: Tc ≈ 9 K in pure β-phase PdH₀.₈₅.

This has been observed in isolated experiments. The specification here makes it reproducible.
