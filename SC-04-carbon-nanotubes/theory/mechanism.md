# Physical Mechanism: (10,10) SWCNT Superconductivity

## The Proximity Effect

The primary mechanism is the superconducting proximity effect. When a normal conductor is placed in intimate contact with a superconductor, Cooper pairs from the superconductor leak into the normal conductor over a characteristic length called the coherence length. If the normal conductor is short enough or clean enough, the entire channel can carry supercurrent.

For a (10,10) armchair SWCNT with NbN contacts (Tc ~ 15 K), the proximity-induced superconductivity in the nanotube channel is governed by:

- The NbN superconducting gap Δ(NbN) ~ 2.3 meV
- The nanotube normal-state coherence length ξN = ħvF/2πkBT
- The contact transparency — how well Cooper pairs inject into the tube

For (10,10) tubes with clean end-bonded contacts, the coherence length exceeds the tube length between contacts, enabling the full proximity effect.

## Why (10,10) Armchair Specifically

(10,10) armchair tubes are always metallic due to their electronic structure — two linear bands crossing at the Fermi point with no gap. Other chiralities are either semiconducting (gap at Fermi level, blocks supercurrent) or have smaller Fermi velocities that reduce the coherence length.

The (10,10) tube has:
- Fermi velocity vF ≈ 8 × 10⁵ m/s
- Two conducting channels at the Fermi level
- No energy gap to suppress supercurrent

## Why Isotopic Purity Matters

C-13 atoms (1.1% in natural carbon) create mass disorder in the nanotube lattice. This disorder scatters phonons and reduces the electron mean free path. For proximity-induced superconductivity, the electron mean free path must exceed the contact spacing for the effect to be observed.

At 99.99% C-12, isotopic scattering is eliminated. Combined with defect-free growth, the mean free path is limited only by the tube length — achieving the ballistic limit.

## Why Superconducting Contacts Are Required

Normal metal contacts (Au, Pd, Ti) create a resistance at the interface due to Fermi surface mismatch and surface contamination. This contact resistance appears in series with the tube and prevents observation of zero resistance even when the tube channel itself is clean.

Pb/NbN contacts have zero resistance below Tc = 15 K. The interface between a superconductor and a ballistic metallic nanotube is Andreev-transparent — Cooper pairs inject directly without barrier. The entire device — contacts plus channel — exhibits zero resistance below 15 K.

## Connection to Established Physics

Proximity-induced superconductivity in carbon nanotubes has been observed experimentally in multiple prior works using normal superconducting contacts. The effect is established. What has not been done is combining isotopically pure C-12 tubes of controlled (10,10) chirality with NbN contacts operating at Tc = 15 K. The three improvements together push the effect into the regime where acceptance criteria can be met reproducibly.
